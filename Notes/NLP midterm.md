NLP 2026. Midterm Grading Rules and Tickets

Exam rules

- Offline exam
    
- A student gets a ticket and has 10-15 minutes to prepare an oral answer (e.g., make notes) then
    
- The student answers a TA/PI for 10 minutes and receives a grade (on a scale 0 to 4).
    

  

Grading rubrics 

0 - no answer / no show

1 - clear written answer + oral answer with major mistake(s) or no oral answer

2 - clear written answer + oral answer with some minor flaw(s)

3 - clear written answer + clear oral answer 

4 - clear written answer + clear oral answer + correct answer to an extra question from TA/PI

  

Rubrics to points: 0 is 0 points, 1 is 5 points, 2 is 10 points, 3 is 15 points, 4 is 20 points

  

Tickets:

  

1. Definition of language, examples. Natural vs. programming languages. Natural language processing, challenges.
    
2. Text tokenization, examples of tokens. Lemmatization vs. stemming. Vocabulary. Zipf’s law. Heaps’ (Herdan’s) law.
    
3. Byte-pair Encoding (BPE) tokenizer. WordPiece tokenizer. Unigram and SentencePiece tokenizer.
    
4. Language modeling. N-gram language models. Sampling methods: temperature sampling, top-k sampling, top-p sampling. Extrinsic vs. intrinsic evaluation of language models, examples of metrics.
    
5. Neural language models: FFN, RNN, Transformer
    
6. Word embeddings. Sparse embeddings, examples. Dense embeddings, examples. How word similarity may be computed.
    
7. Word2Vec, fastText, GloVe models for word embeddings. Extrinsic vs. intrinsic evaluation of word embedding models, examples of metrics.
    
8. Part-of-speech (POS) tagging. Conditional Random Fields (CRFs), Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) for POS tagging. Viterbi algorithm. 
    
9. Syntax. Grammar. Constituency parsing, algorithms. Dependency parsing, algorithms.
    
10.  Knowledge graphs (KGs), examples. Ontology. Wordnet. How to build a knowledge graph. KG embeddings, models.
